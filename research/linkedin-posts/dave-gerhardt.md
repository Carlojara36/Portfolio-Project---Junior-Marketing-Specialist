# Dave Gerhardt — LinkedIn Posts

## Post 1: "Nobody Goes to School for B2B Marketing"
- **Date:** 2021-2025 (Exit Five founding narrative)
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/davegerhardt
- **Summary:** This was the founding insight behind Exit Five and the thesis Dave used to build a $3M+ community. B2B marketing has no formal education path, so Exit Five filled the gap. This positioning made Exit Five the obvious destination for B2B marketers.
- **Key Takeaway:** Name the gap in education/market and position your community or content as the solution. The problem statement IS your positioning.

## Post 2: LinkedIn as PR Channel
- **Date:** 2024-2025
- **Source:** https://community.inc/article/exit-five
- **Summary:** Dave argues that LinkedIn should be the primary PR channel for any B2B startup. Exit Five drives ~60% of its 5,700+ paid member signups from LinkedIn. No PR firm, no press releases — just consistent LinkedIn content across multiple team accounts.
- **Key Takeaway:** LinkedIn is not a social channel for B2B — it's your PR department. Treat it as your primary earned media channel.

## Post 3: The Omnipresence Strategy
- **Date:** 2024-2025
- **Source:** https://community.inc/article/exit-five
- **Summary:** Dave has multiple Exit Five team members posting on LinkedIn daily, creating an "omnipresence" effect where the brand feels like it's everywhere. Combined following of ~300K across accounts, 2M+ monthly impressions. The audience sees Exit Five from multiple angles.
- **Key Takeaway:** One founder posting is good. Three team members posting is exponential. The sum > individual parts because people see you "everywhere."

## Post 4: Personal Page > Company Page
- **Date:** 2025-2026
- **Source:** https://www.exitfive.com/podcast/b2b-social-media-strategy-discussion
- **Summary:** Dave emphasizes that personal profiles vastly outperform company pages on LinkedIn. He advises B2B brands to invest in getting their executives and team members posting from personal accounts rather than publishing from the company page.
- **Key Takeaway:** Company pages reach ~1-2% of followers. Personal profiles reach 10-20x more. The ROI on getting your CEO to post is higher than any company page campaign.

## Post 5: Curate, Don't Create
- **Date:** 2023-2025
- **Source:** https://community.inc/article/exit-five
- **Summary:** Exit Five's content model is "curate, don't create" — they leverage conversations from the community, podcast clips, and member insights rather than producing entirely original content. This scales content output with a small team.
- **Key Takeaway:** Your community already creates valuable content. Curate and amplify it. Original creation is slow; curation is scalable.

## Post 6: Founder-Led Community Flywheel
- **Date:** 2020-2025
- **Source:** https://www.siegemedia.com/conversation/how-dave-gerhardt-built-a-b2b-audience-of-200000
- **Summary:** Dave grew from a solo LinkedIn poster to building a community that now does its own member recruitment through referrals (~20% of growth). Happy members refer peers, which creates more content, which drives more signups. The founder starts the flywheel; the community sustains it.
- **Key Takeaway:** Early stage: founder builds audience. Growth stage: community recruits itself. The transition is the unlock.

## Post 7: Making Reluctant Executives Post
- **Date:** 2026
- **Source:** https://www.exitfive.com/podcast/b2b-social-media-strategy-discussion
- **Summary:** In a panel discussion, Dave addressed how to get executives who resist posting to start. His advice: start with commenting and sharing others' posts (lowest friction), graduate to sharing your own content, and eventually to original posting. Meet executives where they are.
- **Key Takeaway:** Don't force executives to become creators overnight. Start with engagement, then sharing, then original posting. The bar for "good enough" is lower than they think.
