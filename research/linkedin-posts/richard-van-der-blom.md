## Post 1: Reach Is Down 60% — And That's Intentional
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Summary:** Richard shares data from his 2026 Algorithm Insights Report: reach is down 60% for active creators over two years, but engagement is only down 20-25%. This means more engagement per view — LinkedIn is delivering on its promise of more relevant reach.
- **Key Takeaway:** Don't panic about reach drops. Higher engagement per view means better quality audience targeting.

## Post 2: Topic Fingerprinting Is the New Priority
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Summary:** Richard explains that LinkedIn now assigns every creator a "topic fingerprint" based on what they post, engage with, and save. Creators who scatter content across unrelated topics confuse the system. Stay focused on 2-3 closely related topics.
- **Key Takeaway:** Choose one main topic and 2-3 subtopics. Aim for 80% of content around those topics to build topic authority.

## Post 3: Commenting on the Wrong Posts Hurts Your Reach
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Summary:** Richard reveals that LinkedIn's interest graph now tracks where you comment. Commenting on off-topic posts dilutes your topic fingerprint and reduces distribution for your own content. He recommends 25-30 minutes of daily on-topic commenting.
- **Key Takeaway:** Every comment shapes your interest graph. Only comment on posts within your expertise zone.

## Post 4: Newsletters Outperform Regular Posts 5x
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Summary:** Richard shares data showing LinkedIn newsletters generate significantly more reach, engagement, and conversion than regular posts. He reports 80% of his conversion (to paid products) comes from his newsletter, not his posts.
- **Key Takeaway:** Start a LinkedIn newsletter. It's the highest-converting format on the platform.

## Post 5: Optimal Posting Frequency: 2-4x Per Week
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Summary:** Richard's research shows the sweet spot is 2-4 posts per week. Moving from once to 2-4 adds ~1,234 impressions per post on average. Daily posting shows a modest total reach increase but a 26% drop in average reach per post.
- **Key Takeaway:** Post less, make each post count. Topic quality > frequency.

## Post 6: Revamp Your Profile to Re-Teach the Algorithm
- **Date:** February 2026
- **Summary:** When Richard saw the raw data from his report, he completely rewrote his LinkedIn profile for the first time in 3 years. He removed old resume items and aimed it at his ideal client. Within 3 weeks, he saw an uptick in reach.
- **Key Takeaway:** Your profile is your landing page. Update it to reflect current positioning, not past roles.

## Post 7: 80% of First Comments Are AI-Written
- **Date:** June 2026
- **Summary:** Richard notes that 80% of the comments he receives in the first 5 minutes of posting are AI-written. This is a signal of how polluted the platform has become with automation.
- **Key Takeaway:** Authentic, human engagement is becoming a competitive advantage as AI noise increases.
