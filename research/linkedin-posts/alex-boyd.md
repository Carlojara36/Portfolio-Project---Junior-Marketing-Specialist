## Post 1: LinkedIn as an Acquisition Channel
- **Date:** ~2019 (the original $200K client story; RevenueZen founded ~2017)
- **Source:** Databox interview (May 2021), Meet Lea article (April 2026)
- **Summary:** A prospect saw Alex in his LinkedIn feed, emailed him, and became a $200K/year client. This serendipitous moment made him realize LinkedIn was a full-fledged acquisition channel.
- **Key Takeaway:** The most valuable LinkedIn content doesn't pitch — it informs. Buyers come to you.

## Post 2: Revenue Attribution Rigor
- **Date:** May 7, 2021 (Databox "Metrics and Chill" podcast)
- **Source:** https://databox.com/metrics-and-chill-revenuezen
- **Summary:** Alex describes tagging every lead source and integrating accounting with Salesforce to track revenue by channel. Revealed LinkedIn generated 34% of total revenue.
- **Key Takeaway:** You can't optimize what you don't measure. Revenue attribution per post is rare and powerful.

## Post 3: The Curiosity Gap in Case Studies
- **Date:** May 7, 2021 (same Databox interview)
- **Source:** https://databox.com/metrics-and-chill-revenuezen
- **Summary:** Alex's technique: write a case study about the attitude that drove results and the outcome, but leave out exactly how. Readers comment "I'll bite — how'd you do it?" creating engagement and warm leads.
- **Key Takeaway:** Create a gap between what you reveal and what you hold back. Curiosity drives conversation.

## Post 4: Balancing Connection and Conversion
- **Date:** May 7, 2021 (Databox interview)
- **Source:** https://databox.com/metrics-and-chill-revenuezen
- **Summary:** Two keys: balance connecting vs converting, and follow-up. "If you only try to convert all the time, it'll fatigue people. If you only let people get to know you, you have a fanbase with no revenue."
- **Key Takeaway:** Mix content types. Educational posts build audience; curiosity-gap case studies convert it.

## Post 5: Three Content Formats That Generate Pipeline
- **Date:** April 8, 2026 (Meet Lea article) / July 2023 (Apollo.io article)
- **Source:** https://meet-lea.com/en/success-stories/alex-boyd + https://www.apollo.io/magazine/wbs-alex-boyd
- **Summary:** Alex identifies the three formats: Tool/method posts, Client result posts, Sharp opinion posts.
- **Key Takeaway:** Every post designed for a specific buyer, not for maximum likes.

## Post 6: Measuring $13K Per Post
- **Date:** July 26, 2023 (Apollo.io article)
- **Source:** https://www.apollo.io/magazine/wbs-alex-boyd
- **Summary:** Alex reveals his LinkedIn posts average $13,000 in revenue per post based on rigorous attribution.
- **Key Takeaway:** When you know the dollar value per post, you stop treating content as a cost center.

---

## Patterns Identified
1. **Attribution-first mindset** — Every lead source is tracked; content ROI is calculated
2. **B2B buyer focus** — Content targets decision-makers, not the LinkedIn content creator community
3. **Curiosity as a conversion lever** — Case studies with deliberate information gaps drive DMs
4. **Low follower count, high conversion** — 30K followers generated $4.5M; proves niche targeting beats reach
5. **Consistency without scale** — Regular posting spread over years, not aggressive daily output
