## Post 1: 24 Things I Can't Stop Thinking About
- **Date:** April 2025
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/brendanhufford_the-next-saas-marketing-playbook-will-make-activity-7322261996870017024-ycwe
- **Engagement:** 35+ comments
- **Summary:** Brendan shares 24 predictions about the future of SaaS marketing, including: "founders who write win," "newsletters are awareness funnels disguised as media brands," "the best SEO strategy is naming the problem, not ranking for the solution," "AI won't kill content marketing, it will kill content without IP."
- **Key Takeaway:** Have a strong point of view. Pattern recognition posts build authority by showing you see things others miss.

## Post 2: The Problem With Checkbox Marketing
- **Date:** March 9, 2024 (first published on Growth Sprints blog)
- **Source:** https://growthsprints.co/checkbox-marketing/
- **Summary:** Brendan explains "checkbox marketing" — doing weekly emails, daily social posts, and weekly blogs without strategy. Most B2B marketers are "time + channel" marketers, checking boxes instead of driving outcomes.
- **Key Takeaway:** Stop doing "time + channel" marketing. Strategy before execution.

## Post 3: Name the Problem, Not the Category
- **Date:** ~2023–2024 (ongoing framework in his speaking and content)
- **Summary:** The most powerful marketing move is naming the problem you solve, not the category you're in. "Content marketing" is a category. "How to escape checkbox marketing" is a problem.
- **Key Takeaway:** If you can name a problem your audience feels but hasn't articulated, you own that space.

## Post 4: The 3S Method for Content IP
- **Date:** ~2024 (referenced in podcast interviews throughout 2024-2025)
- **Source:** Growth Activated Podcast (Feb 2026); Conversion Alchemy Podcast (May 2024)
- **Summary:** Brendan's 3S framework: interview Sales, Success, and Support teams to find patterns, objections, and language customers use. Use this raw material for content.
- **Key Takeaway:** Your best content ideas already exist in sales calls, support tickets, and customer conversations.

## Post 5: Discovery, Virality, and Relationship Loops
- **Date:** February 24, 2026 (Growth Activated podcast)
- **Source:** https://www.buzzsprout.com/2431600/episodes/18716862
- **Summary:** Brendan's 4-part growth framework: Content IP, Discovery loops (Google/LLMs), Virality loops (executives posting on LinkedIn), Relationship loops (newsletters, podcasts).
- **Key Takeaway:** Most B2B companies only focus on discovery. Real growth comes from adding virality and relationship loops.

## Post 6: LinkedIn Impressions Predict Revenue
- **Date:** 2025–2026 (Systems Digest episode)
- **Source:** https://systemsdigest.com/videos/linkedin-revenue-channel-if-you-know-what-look-brendan-hufford
- **Summary:** Brendan shares an experiment showing direct correlation between LinkedIn impressions and pipeline/revenue.
- **Key Takeaway:** You don't need perfect attribution. Pattern recognition across channels is good enough.

## Patterns Identified
1. **Named concepts** — Brendan creates memorable labels (checkbox marketing, Content IP, 3S method) that stick
2. **SaaS-specific** — Content is explicitly targeted at B2B SaaS companies ($10M-$100M), not general audience
3. **Framework-heavy** — Every insight is packaged as a repeatable framework others can implement
4. **Customer proximity** — Emphasizes getting close to customers (Sales, Success, Support interviews)
5. **Channel diversification** — LinkedIn is one loop in a larger system, not the only channel
