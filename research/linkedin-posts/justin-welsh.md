## Post 1: 4-Step Content Production System

## Post 2: The Trailer-Meat-CTA Framework
- **Date:** ~2021–2023 (ongoing pattern; documented by Josh Spector, Oct 2023)
- **Source:** https://joshspector.com/justin-welsh-linkedin-system/
- **Summary:** Justin's three-part post structure. The Trailer (visible before "see more") breaks the scroll. The Meat delivers the promised value. The CTA drives comments.
- **Key Takeaway:** First line breaks scroll, second line builds tension, last line before "see more" promises payoff.

## Post 3: Solve One Expensive Problem Repeatedly
- **Date:** Ongoing philosophy (documented extensively since ~2022–2023)
- **Source:** https://justinwelsh.me (his website and newsletter)
- **Summary:** Justin's core content philosophy: solve one expensive problem repeatedly. Every piece of content orbits the question "How do you build attention or revenue on your own?"
- **Key Takeaway:** Topic clarity beats content variety. Every post should serve one core positioning.

## Post 4: The Link-Edit Strategy
- **Date:** ~2022–2023 (documented by Josh Spector, Oct 2023; updated in 2025–2026)
- **Source:** https://joshspector.com/justin-welsh-linkedin-system/ + https://autoposting.ai/blog/justin-welsh
- **Summary:** Justin publishes posts without external links, lets them accumulate reach during the algorithm's initial scoring window (first 2-3 hours), then edits to add a business link afterward.
- **Key Takeaway:** LinkedIn penalizes external links (~60% less reach). Publishing clean first, editing later, sidesteps this.

## Post 6: The 30-30-30 Method (originally called 30-for-30)

## Patterns Identified
1. **Consistency over intensity** — Daily posting for years, not viral spikes
2. **System over inspiration** — Templates, batching, and workflows replace creative waiting
3. **Audience-first monetization** — Build trust through value, then monetize through aligned offers
4. **Topic discipline** — Stays inside solopreneurship/personal brand building; rarely strays
5. **Emotional resonance** — Writes to what people feel, not just what they think
