## Post 1: LinkedIn as an Acquisition Channel
- **Date:** Ongoing
- **Summary:** Alex shares how a prospect saw him in their LinkedIn feed, emailed him, and became a $200K/year client. This serendipitous moment made him realize LinkedIn was a full-fledged acquisition channel — not just a place to share ideas.
- **Key Takeaway:** The most valuable LinkedIn content doesn't pitch — it informs. Buyers come to you.

## Post 2: Revenue Attribution Rigor
- **Date:** 2021 (Databox interview)
- **Summary:** Alex describes how he tags every lead source and integrates accounting with Salesforce to track revenue by channel, not just pipeline. This revealed that LinkedIn generated 34% of total revenue.
- **Key Takeaway:** You can't optimize what you don't measure. Revenue attribution per post is rare and powerful.

## Post 3: The Curiosity Gap in Case Studies
- **Date:** 2021
- **Summary:** Alex shares his technique: write a case study talking about the attitude that drove results and the outcome, but leave out exactly how you did it. Readers will comment "I'll bite — how'd you do it?" creating engagement and warm leads.
- **Key Takeaway:** Create a gap between what you reveal and what you hold back. Curiosity drives conversation.

## Post 4: Balancing Connection and Conversion
- **Date:** Various
- **Summary:** Alex emphasizes two keys: striking the right balance between connecting and converting, and follow-up. "If you only try to convert all the time, it'll fatigue people. If you only let people get to know you, you have a fanbase with no revenue."
- **Key Takeaway:** Mix content types. Educational/inspirational posts build the audience; curiosity-gap case studies convert it.

## Post 5: Three Content Formats That Generate Pipeline
- **Date:** 2023-2024
- **Summary:** Alex identifies the formats that work: Tool/method posts (show a precise technique), Client result posts (document anonymized successes), Sharp opinion posts (take a position on a B2B marketing question).
- **Key Takeaway:** Every post should be designed for a specific buyer, not for maximum likes.

## Post 6: Measuring $13K Per Post
- **Date:** 2023
- **Summary:** Alex reveals that his LinkedIn posts average $13,000 in revenue per post, based on rigorous attribution. This changes the ROI calculation entirely — each post is a revenue-generating asset.
- **Key Takeaway:** When you know the dollar value per post, you stop treating content as a cost center.

---

## Patterns Identified
1. **Attribution-first mindset** — Every lead source is tracked; content ROI is calculated
2. **B2B buyer focus** — Content targets decision-makers, not the LinkedIn content creator community
3. **Curiosity as a conversion lever** — Case studies with deliberate information gaps drive DMs
4. **Low follower count, high conversion** — 30K followers generated $4.5M; proves niche targeting beats reach
5. **Consistency without scale** — Regular posting spread over years, not aggressive daily output
