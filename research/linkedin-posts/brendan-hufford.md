## Post 1: 24 Things I Can't Stop Thinking About

- **Key Takeaway:** Have a strong point of view. Pattern recognition posts build authority by showing you see things others miss.

## Post 2: The Problem With Checkbox Marketing
- **Date:** 2024-2025
- **Summary:** Brendan explains "checkbox marketing" — doing weekly emails, daily social posts, and weekly blogs without strategy. He argues that most B2B marketers are "time + channel" marketers, just checking boxes instead of driving outcomes.
- **Key Takeaway:** Stop doing "time + channel" marketing. Strategy before execution.

## Post 3: Name the Problem, Not the Category
- **Date:** 2024
- **Summary:** Brendan argues that the most powerful marketing move is naming the problem you solve, not the category you're in. "Content marketing" is a category. "How to escape checkbox marketing" is a problem. The latter attracts buyers.
- **Key Takeaway:** If you can name a problem your audience feels but hasn't articulated, you own that space.

## Post 4: The 3S Method for Content IP
- **Date:** 2024
- **Summary:** Brendan shares his 3S framework for developing Content IP: interview Sales, Success, and Support teams to find the patterns, objections, and language customers actually use. Use this raw material to build content that resonates.
- **Key Takeaway:** Your best content ideas already exist inside your company — in sales calls, support tickets, and customer conversations.

## Post 5: Discovery, Virality, and Relationship Loops
- **Date:** 2025
- **Summary:** Brendan outlines his 4-part growth framework: Content IP (what you're known for), Discovery loops (getting found in Google/LLMs), Virality loops (executives posting on LinkedIn), and Relationship loops (newsletters, podcasts, webinars).
- **Key Takeaway:** Most B2B companies only focus on discovery. The real growth comes from adding virality and relationship loops.

## Post 6: LinkedIn Impressions Predict Revenue
- **Date:** 2025-2026
- **Summary:** Brendan shares how he ran an experiment showing a direct correlation between LinkedIn impressions and pipeline/revenue. He argues that more GTM leaders should stop obsessing over perfect attribution and start looking for meaningful correlations.
- **Key Takeaway:** You don't need perfect attribution. Pattern recognition across channels is good enough to guide investment.

## Patterns Identified
1. **Named concepts** — Brendan creates memorable labels (checkbox marketing, Content IP, 3S method) that stick
2. **SaaS-specific** — Content is explicitly targeted at B2B SaaS companies ($10M-$100M), not general audience
3. **Framework-heavy** — Every insight is packaged as a repeatable framework others can implement
4. **Customer proximity** — Emphasizes getting close to customers (Sales, Success, Support interviews)
5. **Channel diversification** — LinkedIn is one loop in a larger system, not the only channel
