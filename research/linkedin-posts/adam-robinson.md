# Adam Robinson — LinkedIn Posts

## Post 1: The P&L Post (Monthly Revenue Transparency)
- **Date:** Monthly recurring (e.g., July 2025, March 2026)
- **URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/retentionadam
- **Summary:** Adam publishes his companies' full P&L on LinkedIn every month — revenue, expenses, profit, churn, everything. This radical transparency is his core content differentiator. No other B2B SaaS founder does this.
- **Key Takeaway:** Publishing real numbers (including failures) builds trust no marketing claim can match. Your "disadvantage" (sharing weakness) becomes your moat.

## Post 2: The 6sense Cease & Desist
- **Date:** September 2023
- **Source:** https://www.trueframe.xyz/blog/adam-robinson-linkedin-funnel
- **Summary:** When 6sense sent a cease and desist letter for a critical LinkedIn post, Adam posted the actual letter. It went viral — 1,600 likes, 913 comments, and 1,600 qualified leads through a Google Form. His legal costs ran $300-400K; he says it was the best marketing spend ever.
- **Key Takeaway:** Legal threats can be asymmetric marketing weapons. When a giant attacks a startup publicly, the startup wins sympathy and attention.

## Post 3: "I Don't Write About RB2B"
- **Date:** 2024-2025 (ongoing philosophy)
- **Source:** https://saastock.com/blog/how-adam-robinson-cracked-founder-led-marketing
- **Summary:** ~90% of Adam's LinkedIn posts have zero CTA. No "check out RB2B," no link in comments. He writes about bootstrapping, GTM strategy, and founder psychology. The 1-in-10 posts with a CTA do all the commercial work.
- **Key Takeaway:** Content earns the right to sell. The ratio is ~9 value posts to 1 promotional post. Trust is built before the ask.

## Post 4: The 63-Character Headline Framework
- **Date:** 2024-2026
- **Source:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pApWguWUwy0 (Unf*ck My Startup)
- **Summary:** Adam teaches that your LinkedIn headline is the most important 63 characters on the platform. That's all that shows up in the feed. His example: "Operationalized social inside HubSpot" — immediately tells you what he does and who for.
- **Key Takeaway:** Your headline must answer "What do you do? Who do you help?" in 63 characters. Every word count.

## Post 5: Killing $100K/Month in Paid Ads
- **Date:** 2024
- **Source:** https://founderpath.com/blog/retentioncom-ceo-keynote-adam-robinson
- **Summary:** Adam tested paid ads, then cut ~$100K/month in spend and reported growth accelerated. His LinkedIn founder brand was cheaper and more effective than any paid channel.
- **Key Takeaway:** Founder-led LinkedIn content can replace paid ads when the audience is built before the product launch.

## Post 6: The Three Content Pillars
- **Date:** 2024-2025
- **Source:** https://startupspells.com/p/adam-robinson-linkedin-playbook-30-million-arr-bootstrapped-brand
- **Summary:** Adam's content sits under three consistent pillars: (1) Building in public (monthly P&L), (2) Opinions on B2B go-to-market, (3) Bootstrapping vs VC. Every post fits one of these three buckets.
- **Key Takeaway:** Content pillars create algorithmic clarity. LinkedIn's topic fingerprinting needs to know exactly what you're about.

## Post 7: Audience Before Product
- **Date:** March 2024 (RB2B launch)
- **Source:** https://www.trueframe.xyz/blog/adam-robinson-linkedin-funnel
- **Summary:** Adam posted on LinkedIn for ~18 months before launching RB2B. By launch day, he had 3,000 people on a waitlist. RB2B hit $1M ARR in 16 weeks because the audience was already there.
- **Key Takeaway:** Build the audience 18-24 months before you need it. The product launch is just the harvest.
